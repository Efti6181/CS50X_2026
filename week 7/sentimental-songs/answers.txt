1. Characterizing the audio aura of a listener

Based on the top 100 songs’ data (energy, danceability, valence), we can estimate the listener’s overall mood or “audio aura.”

High energy & danceability: The listener prefers upbeat, active songs.

High valence: The listener enjoys happy, positive songs.

Low energy or valence: The listener prefers calm, mellow, or sad songs.

2. Why this calculation might not be representative

Using only the top 100 songs from a single year may not fully represent the listener’s music taste because:

They may listen to many songs outside of these 100.

Play counts may be skewed by playlists or a few repeated songs.

Different genres or moods might not be reflected accurately in averages.

3. Better ways to calculate the audio aura

Use all songs listened to during the year, not just top 100.

Weight songs by play count to better reflect actual listening habits.

Consider genre and context to capture emotional variety.

Track changes over time to see mood evolution rather than a single static snapshot.
